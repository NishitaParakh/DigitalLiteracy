## Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud is increasing rapidly due to the widespread use of digital payment apps among students and young users.

A typical case involves a college student who receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a customer care executive or a buyer/seller from an online platform. The attacker claims that money needs to be “received” and asks the student to approve a payment request on their UPI app. The steps usually include: 
1. gaining the victim’s trust by using a convincing story.
2. sending a payment request or a fake link
3. instructing the victim to enter their UPI PIN to “receive” money. In reality, entering the PIN authorizes a payment, and money gets transferred to the attacker’s account.

Students and inexperienced users are the most common targets because they may not fully understand how UPI transactions work. The consequences of such fraud include-
1. immediate financial loss
2. difficulty in recovering money
3. potential misuse of personal information.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment systems. Users must understand that money can only be received without entering a PIN, and any request asking for a PIN should be treated as suspicious.
